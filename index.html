Global Suicide Rates

It is estimated that one in 5,000 - 15,000 dies from suicide every year globally resulting in a global suicide rate of 10.5 deaths per 100,000. This results in more than 700,000 people taking their lives globally, every year.
Among 15 - 19 year olds, suicide is the fourth leading cause of death, and 77% of suicides occur in low and middle income countries. Every suicide causes lasting damage that leaves long lasting effects on friends, family, and community.

Average Suicides over Time

There has been shown that there is a significant difference in suicide rates between those who identify as males, and thoses who identify as females. Globally, males are 1.8 time more likely to commit suicide (2018), but in the western world, males suicide rates are 3 to 4 times higher than female suicide rates.
Contrasting to these facts, females are much more likely to have suicidal thoughts, which asks the question why are male suicide rates higher than female rates if females have more suicidal thoughts? This is known as the Suicide Gender Paradox.

Suicides per 100,000 People, per Country, over Time

Note: Each colour group represents ~16% of countries. I.e. the first colour represents the countries that fall into the lowest 1/6 of suicide rates, and the last group represents the countries that fall into the highest 1/6 of suicide rates.

The highest suicide rate recorded since the year 2000 was in Lesotho, a country completed encapsulated by South Africa, with a suicide rate of 92.6 suicides per 100,000 people. Contrasting to this, both Saint Vincent and the Grenadines and Antigua and Barbuda both have several reported years with zero suicides.
Other notable countries include Russia, which consistently has a very high suicide rate, Australia, New Zealand, USA and China, which all hold a slightly higher than average suicide rates, and finally Cyprus, which has the lowest suicide rate in Europe, the continent with the highest average suicide rate.
